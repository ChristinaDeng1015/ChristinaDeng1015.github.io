STORY OF CHRISTINA

This October 2018, She turned 23 with a sudden sense of loss and anxiousness, unsure with her directions of life. Like other new grads, she was vigorously seeking for opportunities, in either education or occupations. She received invitation into Master’s of Big Data, but a UX designer friend recommended Digital Media for her Master. It was something different; a big change that will be out of her comfort zone. She accepted her master’s offer and declined the final round of interview with the HSBC manager.
First semester of the Master program taught her a lot of things that she had never experienced before. She improved her presentation skills and been able to work with groups. She understood how companies actually ran and gained a lot more practical experience. Gradually, She found her passion and things she wanted to do.
She was working during winter break as sales associate and style advisor and she realized life could be so simple. And jobs and degrees were not the things that defined success. Keeping learning and finding the passion were more important than anything else.
